But my secret life is one immersed in art.
Art is my life and always has been. Not pop art. Not in your face, checkmark, social proof seeking art.

The intangible art all around us. The art that we escape into when seeking solace and comfort. The place we transport to when in 'flow' and deep in a trance in the activity at hand.

In recent years, I have been shedding those layers of secrecy. I will continue to build. I will continue to make. I will proudly boast that my products HAVE BEEN AND ARE Art. A fully immersed life of Art has always been my ambition.

Why Amiga?

Well, the Amiga wasn't my first experience with a computer (that would be a massive Fortran machine at my mom's job in the late 70s, and then after that, we had a TRS-80 at home), but it was the first computer where I would spend endless hours playing and learning on. It made me the 'cool kid' where, before getting to high school and distracted by so many other things, my friends and I would spend endless hours, months, and even years creating music, playing games, and learning how to program.

In recent years as I've been jumping back in deep into my creative world, I have had moments of sheer joy which has reminded me of those Amiga days.
